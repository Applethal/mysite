Artists - independent of context - love to experiment. To provide fresh commodities, creatives explore their search space, which is sadly often constrained by a set of rules indoctronated by the human race. In the context of food, the theoritical search space is finite, it is subject to the raw resources of the earth. As of yet, and at the best of my knowledge, the following defines the upper bound: \[ \prod_{i=1}^{11} X_i \] Let \(X_i\) be the decision variable that expresses the quantity of using a raw material.

1. \(X_1 : M \quad (\text{Meat})\)
2. \(X_2 : F \quad (\text{Fish})\)
3. \(X_3 : V \quad (\text{Vegetables})\)
4. \(X_4 : FR \quad (\text{Fruits})\)
5. \(X_5 : G \quad (\text{Grains})\)
6. \(X_6 : D \quad (\text{Dairy})\)
7. \(X_7 : L \quad (\text{Legumes})\)
8. \(X_8 : NS \quad (\text{Nuts and Seeds})\)
9. \(X_9 : H \quad (\text{Herbs})\)
10. \(X_{10} : S \quad (\text{Spices})\)
11. \(X_{11} : B \quad (\text{Beverages})\)

With respect to: \[ X_i \geq 0 \quad \text{for } i = 1, 2, \ldots, 11 \] It is basically a combination of every thing that I could think of, maybe there is another categorie to speak of but you get the gist of it. Still, researches came up with a long list of restrictions that introduce cuts to make room for some feasibilty, you can't of course mix pepsi and milk or else you would end up with a magical potion. Health is sacred and so is the awareness!
